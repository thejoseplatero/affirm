The full terms

Every financing product has terms. Here are mine: direct fit, real adjacency, and the one gap I'd rather you hear from me.

Direct fit

Leading a PM team through managers, at scale. 45+ people across four practices, a manager layer I hired and developed. The status program relaunch for 9M+ members, the biggest change to the program since its last relaunch, was run day to day by one of my senior managers. My job was the operating system it ran on and the air cover it needed. That's the muscle "operate through layers of leadership" actually requires.

Direct fit

Agentic, AI-native product development. Not a familiarity claim. A shipped system. I built and run the operating model where agents draft PRDs and user stories, prep executive reporting, audit initiatives, and plan capacity, in daily use across my team. This page itself came out of that system, and the agents run live at joseplatero.com: watch them compile a brief, walk a Jira board, and file a note while you read.

Direct fit

A homepage that outgrew its own navigation. Air Canada's highest-traffic page had outgrown flights-only. I rebuilt the navigation to convert and to sell more than flights, a Webby Honoree. Same shape as a Home/Search/Profile portfolio that needs a rethink, not a redesign.

Direct fit

Two-sided marketplace, run daily. A loyalty program is one: 9M+ members on one side, the airline plus Uber, Starbucks, Marriott, and bank partners on the other, competing for the same pages. Balancing merchant value against what the member actually sees is not a concept I read about. It is the negotiation I run every quarter, inside Home-equivalent real estate.

Direct fit

"Clarity when things feel messy." That is the job I already do. The homepage mandate arrived as noise: brand complaints, a bug-fix crew, no team. I banned the word redesign, reframed it as a rethink with three crisp problems, and shipped it to a Webby. Today an agent-built weekly brief turns four portfolios of noise into one narrative my executives actually read. Messy in, crisp out, repeatedly.

Adjacent, not identical

Commerce monetization, not payments. I put targeted co-brand credit card offers inside booking, ancillary, and loyalty flows: a new revenue line on pages customers were already on, and stood up a net-new adtech function, team and commercial model, from scratch. A real track record monetizing a page people already trust. Not payments or lending, and I won't pretend otherwise.

The gap

LTV/CAC-driven prioritization. My track record is at the engagement and top-line layer: conversion, activation, revenue lines, not unit-economics modeling. If that rigor is non-negotiable on day one, I'd rather say so now than let you discover it in month two.
